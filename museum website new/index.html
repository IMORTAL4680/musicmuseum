Music Museum of Nepal

The Music Museum of Nepal was founded in the year 1995 with a view of collecting, preserving and glorifying Nepali folk musical instruments. There are more than 100 ethnic groups in Nepal, each with their own culture and traditions to mark every occasion from birth to death with music. Each group organizes various musical ceremonies and plays their own musical instruments in accordance with traditions and rituals. Many groups such as Gaine, Damai, Badi have adopted folk music as their way of life and play folk musical instruments professionally. Our study has found more than 1350 types of musical instruments in Nepal.

Discover the Harmonious Tapestry of Nepal's Musical Heritage

The Music Museum of Nepal, located in the bustling heart of Kathmandu, is a hidden gem that unveils the captivating story of the nation's musical history and culture. As you step across its threshold, you are transported into a world where time seems to stand still, and the echoes of centuries past resonate in every note, every instrument, and every artifact.

Our Mission

At the Music Museum of Nepal, our mission is to preserve, celebrate, and share the rich musical traditions of Nepal with the world. We are dedicated to showcasing the country's unique and diverse musical heritage while fostering an appreciation for the artistry, history, and craftsmanship behind the instruments and melodies.
